What's New in Lunar & Planetary Science

The CNSA (Chinese National Space Administration) Chang'e 5 sample return vehicle has been recovered and the sample container has returned 1.73 kg of lunar regolith. It reached Earth on December 16. At 17:59 UT (12:59 pm EST, 1:59 a.m. Beijing time Dec. 17) making a nighttime landing in China.

The sample return capsule from the JAXA (Japanese Space Agency) Hayabusa2 mission, carrying material from the asteroid Ryugu, reentered the atmosphere and landed in the Woomera Test Range in Australia at about 4:17 a.m. December 6 Australian Central Daylight time (17:47 UT Dec. 5 ; 12:47 p.m Dec. 5 EST). The capsule has been recovered. The Hayabusa2 bus flew past Earth successfully as planned to begin its extended mission.

The OSIRIS-REx mission performed its touch-and-go (TAG) maneuver to retrieve a sample from the asteroid Bennu on October 20. The 4 1/2 hour operation culminated with OSIRIS-REx descending to the Nightingale region and using its sampling arm to conduct the TAG operation at roughly 22:08 UT Earth Received Time (6:08 p.m. EDT). Images of the sampler head indicate a good sample was obtained, and the sample has now been stowed in the sample return capsule. For more, see the NASA Press Release.
